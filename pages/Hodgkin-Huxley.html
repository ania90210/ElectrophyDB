The Hodgkin-Huxley Model

First mathematical model describing the behavior of membrane in excitable cells was presented by Alan Hodgkin and Andrew Huxley in 1952. They developed the electronic circuit to model the propagation of action potential in the squid giant axon. This model treats every component of excitable cell as an electrical unit.

The lipid bilayer has a capacitance Cm, voltage-gated ion channels are represented by conductance gx, where x is a specific ion and gL describes linear conductance of leak ion channels. Ex represents the electrochemical gradient that drives the flow of ion x and Iapp is the applied stimulus current.
